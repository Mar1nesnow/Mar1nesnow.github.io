📍 Coon Rapids, Iowa

Welcome! I’m an environmental scientist with a passion for understanding ecosystems through both data and lived experience.

In 2025, I earned dual degrees in Biology (Marine, Ecology, and Evolution) and Gender, Sexuality, and Women’s Studies from Bowdoin College in Maine. I seek an interdisciplinary foundation that shapes how I approach environmental work as both science and story.

My research experience spans sea star behavior, oyster aquaculture, and marine protist culturing, with experience designing, conducting, and presenting projects.

In my recent experience with Texas Parks and Wildlife - Coastal Fisheries, I worked on permitting processes, public engagement, and fisheries sampling, gaining insight into applied conservation at the state level.

I currently serve as an AmeriCorps member at Whiterock Conservancy in Iowa, where I focus on land stewardship through invasive species removal, soil and water sampling, environmental education for diverse audiences, and trail / campground maintenance.

I value preparation and safety in the field, but I also believe curiosity and connection to place are just as essential. Moving forward, I’m eager to deepen my skills in data analysis, programming, scientific communication, and grant writing alongside hands-on environmental work.
